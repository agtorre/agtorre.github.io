Google Survey Oops

Thu, Oct 1, 2015

Yesterday, a Google employee made a silly mistake that revealed some unexpected insights.

It started when I received a strange email asking for feedback on one of their coaching program that I had participated in long ago while interviewing. This email was unusual because it skipped my filter, but appeared to be spam at first glance. The title was: “{Action Requested} Google Candidate Coaching Survey”. Even more strangely, the sender used CC for the mailing list revealing 481 email addresses!

Soon after receiving it, people began using reply-all. There was plenty of light-hearted discussion as well including spam complaints and recruiting attempts. After several replies, a trend appeared – the majority of individuals had ethnic-sounding names. This observation was brought up in the thread and soon others pointed out you could look at G+ photos from Gmail and that people largely appeared to be ethnic. In total, I received 42 replies and everyone seemed to acknowledge this observation, or at least no one dismissed it.

The whole thing was ridiculous and I feel bad for the Google employee who sent it. The lingering questions in my head are:

  1. Does Google keep a mailing list of ethnic people?
  2. Were these people targeted for this coaching program?
